Pasta Amatriciana

Description

Pasta Amatriciana is one of the lesser known roman pastas, related to and just as simple as the Carbonara.

Like the Carbonara it only requires few ingredients, and relies more on temperature and a good cook on the pasta.

Ingredients

  • Pasta
  • Canned whole tomatoes
  • Pecorino Romano
  • Guanciale
  • White wine
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the guanciale by dicing it in 2.5mm chunks.
  2. Fry the quanciale on medium heat. You can start the pan cold, as the fat will render plenty of grease for sticking not to be an issue.
  3. While the guanciale is frying put on a large pot of boiling water for the pasta.
  4. Fry until fat is rendered. Remove the guanciale but leave the pan on the heat.
  5. Pour the tomato in a blender and blend briefly.
  6. Once the water is boiling, add a bunch of salt to the water and add the pasta. Boil according to the package instructions, but subtract a few minutes, as the pasta will cook on in the pan later.
  7. Pour the tomato sauce in the pan, add a large dash of wine, and simmer while the pasta is boiling.
  8. Grate the pecorino cheese.
  9. Add the pasta to the pan with the guanciale, and a ladle of pasta water, add the pecorino and stir on low heat until only slightly runny.
  10. Serve the dish. Bon appetit.
