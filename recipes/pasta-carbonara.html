Pasta Carbonara

Description

Pasta Carbonara is one of those dishes everyone's tried making, but most haven't mastered. It's rumoured to be difficult, but really it's not.

What it requires is just a little pasta water, a bit of bravery, and a lot of temperature control

Ingredients

  • Pasta
  • Eggs
  • Pecorino Romano
  • Guanciale
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the guanciale by dicing it in 2.5mm chunks.
  2. Fry the quanciale on medium heat. You can start the pan cold, as the fat will render plenty of grease for sticking not to be an issue.
  3. While the guanciale is frying put on a large pot of boiling water for the pasta.
  4. Fry until fat is rendered. Turn the heat off after, but leave the pan on.
  5. Beat the eggs. One whole egg and several yolks. About one yolk per 100g of pasta.
  6. Once the water is boiling, add a bunch of salt to the water and add the pasta. Boil according to the package instructions, but subtract a few minutes, as the pasta will cook on in the pan later.
  7. Grate the pecorino cheese and add that to the egg mix. Add a bunch of pepper here.
  8. Add the pasta to the pan with the guanciale, let it cool until it's barely steaming and add the egg mix.
  9. Stir and heat on very low heat until no longer runny.
  10. Serve the dish. Bon appetit.
